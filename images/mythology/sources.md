## Detailed Source Information

### Griffin
- **Full Title:** Panel with a Griffin
- **Origin:** Byzantine (Greece or Balkans)
- **Date:** 1250-1300
- **Medium:** Marble
- **Collection:** Metropolitan Museum of Art, Gallery 303
- **Met Object ID:** 472849
- **Notes:** Exceptionally fine example of Late Byzantine stone carving. Griffin depicted within a roundel, resembling Byzantine silk designs.

### Phoenix
- **Full Title:** Aberdeen Bestiary, Folio 56r - The Phoenix
- **Origin:** England
- **Date:** c. 1200
- **Medium:** Illuminated manuscript on parchment
- **Collection:** University of Aberdeen Library (MS 24)
- **Direct Access:** https://www.abdn.ac.uk/bestiary/ms24/f56r
- **Notes:** Shows phoenix turning to face the sun, beating wings to fan flames. Symbol of resurrection. Alternative: Wikimedia Commons has Aberdeen Bestiary images in Category:Aberdeen_Bestiary.

### Dragon
- **Full Title:** Dragon
- **Origin:** Spanish (Castile monastery)
- **Date:** After 1200
- **Medium:** Fresco mounted on canvas
- **Dimensions:** 10 ft. 11 in. x 11 ft.
- **Collection:** Metropolitan Museum of Art, The Cloisters, Gallery 01
- **Met Object ID:** 471062
- **Notes:** Counterpart to opposing lion fresco. Monastery abandoned 1841.

### Unicorn
- **Full Title:** The Unicorn Rests in a Garden (from the Unicorn Tapestries)
- **Origin:** French (cartoon) / South Netherlandish (woven)
- **Date:** 1495-1505
- **Medium:** Wool warp with wool, silk, silver, and gilt wefts
- **Dimensions:** 12 ft. x 8 ft. 3 in.
- **Collection:** Metropolitan Museum of Art, The Cloisters, Gallery 17
- **Met Object ID:** 467642
- **Provenance:** Gift of John D. Rockefeller Jr., 1937
- **Notes:** May have been created as a single image. Unicorn tethered but not securely - represents "the beloved tamed."

### Kraken
- **Full Title:** Carta Marina (Marine Map)
- **Origin:** Swedish
- **Artist:** Olaus Magnus
- **Date:** 1539
- **Medium:** Woodcut map
- **Collection:** James Ford Bell Library, University of Minnesota
- **Wikimedia File:** File:Carta_Marina.jpeg
- **Notes:** Historic map featuring numerous sea monsters. Category:Olaus_Magnus on Wikimedia Commons contains "Kraken size (Olaus Magnus).png" as alternative.

### Sphinx
- **Full Title:** Sphinx of Hatshepsut
- **Origin:** Egyptian, from Thebes (Deir el-Bahri)
- **Date:** c. 1479-1458 BCE (Dynasty 18, New Kingdom)
- **Medium:** Granite with traces of paint
- **Collection:** Metropolitan Museum of Art
- **Met Object ID:** 544442
- **Notes:** Colossal sphinx depicting female pharaoh with lion body and human head wearing nemes-headcloth.
- **Alternatives:** Sphinx of Senwosret III (544186), Sphinx of Thutmose III (544475)

### Pegasus
- **Full Title:** Terracotta lekythos (oil flask)
- **Origin:** Greek, Attic
- **Artist:** Attributed to the Diosphos Painter
- **Date:** c. 500 BCE
- **Medium:** Terracotta, black-figure
- **Collection:** Metropolitan Museum of Art
- **Met Object ID:** 247488
- **Notes:** Depicts Perseus flying with Medusa's head while Pegasus springs from her severed neck.
- **Alternatives:** Odilon Redon "Pegasus and Bellerophon" (459400); Corinthian stater coins on Wikimedia Commons (Category:Pegasus_on_coins)

### Chimera
- **Full Title:** Chimera of Arezzo (Chimera d'Arezzo)
- **Origin:** Etruscan
- **Date:** c. 400 BCE
- **Medium:** Bronze
- **Collection:** Museo Archeologico Nazionale, Florence
- **Wikimedia Category:** Category:Chimera_of_Arezzo
- **Sample File:** File:Chimera_d'arezzo,_fi,_01.JPG
- **Notes:** One of the most famous Etruscan bronzes. Lion body, goat head on back, snake tail. Found in Arezzo.

### Leviathan
- **Full Title:** The Destruction of Leviathan
- **Artist:** Gustave Dore
- **Date:** c. 1866
- **Medium:** Engraving
- **Collection:** Public Domain (originally from Dore Bible)
- **Wikimedia File:** File:Destruction_of_Leviathan.png
- **Resolution:** 2,288 x 2,890 pixels
- **Notes:** Illustration of Isaiah 27:1. God descends from clouds with sword as Leviathan rises from the sea.

## Notes on AI Generation

All nine mythological creatures have excellent public domain historical art available. No AI generation required.

**Strongest sources (highest artistic quality):**
1. Unicorn - The Met's Unicorn Tapestries are masterworks of medieval art
2. Sphinx - Ancient Egyptian artifact with tremendous gravitas
3. Chimera - The Arezzo bronze is iconic ancient art
4. Dragon - Large-scale medieval Spanish fresco
5. Griffin - Byzantine marble relief with fine detail

**Good alternatives if needed:**
- Phoenix: British Library digitized bestiaries (Public Domain mark)
- Dragon: British Library medieval manuscripts (Egerton MS, Royal MS collections)
- Kraken: Multiple sea monsters from Carta Marina details
- Pegasus: Corinthian stater coins (5th-4th century BCE)
