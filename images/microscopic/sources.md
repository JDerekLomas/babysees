## Primary Source Libraries

### Government Sources (Public Domain)
- **NIAID Flickr**: https://www.flickr.com/photos/niaid/ - Stunning colorized electron microscopy, all public domain
- **CDC PHIL**: https://phil.cdc.gov/ - 17,000+ public health images, searchable by subject
- **NCI Visuals Online**: https://visualsonline.cancer.gov/ - 3,559 images, mostly public domain
- **NIH BioArt Source**: https://bioart.niaid.nih.gov/discover - Free biomedical illustrations
- **NASA SVS**: https://svs.gsfc.nasa.gov/ - Scientific visualizations including microscopy

### Academic/Community Sources
- **Cell Image Library**: https://www.cellimagelibrary.org/ - Curated microscopy database, many CC0/public domain
- **Wikimedia Commons**: https://commons.wikimedia.org/ - Massive collection, check individual licenses
- **Wellcome Collection**: https://wellcomecollection.org/ - 100,000+ images under CC BY 4.0
- **Dartmouth EM Facility**: https://www.dartmouth.edu/emlab/gallery/ - Classic pollen/biology SEM images

## Notes on AI Generation Recommendations

The following concepts may benefit from AI-generated imagery due to limited dramatic public domain options:

| Concept | Reason | Recommendation |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| **Atom** | Cannot be photographed - all images are artistic models/diagrams | AI generation recommended for a visually striking, toddler-appropriate atom visualization |
| **DNA** | Most public domain options are diagrams rather than dramatic 3D renders | AI generation could produce more visually compelling helix imagery |
| **Neuron** | Beautiful fluorescence images exist but may be too complex visually | Consider AI for simplified, colorful neuron with clear dendrite branches |

## Best Picks for Toddler Picture Book

### Highest Visual Impact (Public Domain):

1. **Snowflake** - Wilson Bentley's images are timeless, crystal-clear, and mesmerizing
2. **Pollen** - Dartmouth's colorized SEM looks genuinely alien and wonderful
3. **Virus (SARS-CoV-2)** - NIAID's colorized images are dramatic and beautiful
4. **Bacteria (MRSA)** - Golden clusters against dark backgrounds, visually striking
5. **Bacteriophage T4** - The "lunar lander" shape is fascinating and otherworldly
6. **Cell (HeLa)** - Fluorescence microscopy with vivid colors (cyan, magenta, blue)

### May Need AI Enhancement:
- **Atom** - No real photographs possible; diagrams lack visual drama
- **Chromosome** - Karyotypes are scientifically accurate but visually flat
- **DNA** - SVG diagrams are clear but lack the dramatic 3D quality desired
